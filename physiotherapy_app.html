Physiotherapy App

UIUX Designer - Research / Flow / Prototyping

scroll

How might we help elders rehabilitate directly at home?

This project involves developing an app for the company's physical therapy clinic. Considering the challenges of public outings, especially during times of inconvenience, this product offers a convenient solution. It allows users to perform simple self-assessments and receive tailored rehabilitation guidance. The app also provides clear instructions and user action examples to ensure users can comfortably engage in home-based treatment.

Flow

Roles

Researcher / Commuication / Prototype
In my role within the RD team at the company, I collaborated closely with the PM to emphasize usability as a priority. This involved conducting patient interviews to uncover their specific needs and preferences. Additionally, I engaged with physiotherapists to ensure the app's functionality aligned with the requirements.
To ensure seamless functionality, I worked in tandem with our back-end engineers, discussing API logic and functionality before transitioning to the creation of app prototypes based on wireframes. This meticulous approach helped establish a strong foundation for the project's development.

Wireframe

Functions & Advantages

Create an useful app for elders.

  • Developed rehabilitation routines tailored for elderly individuals:
    Physiotherapists create personalized exercise regimens for patients to follow at home. The app schedules exercises and sends timely reminders to users.
  • Implemented a self-assessment feature for users to gauge their pain levels:
    We designed a set of questions for users to answer, which then guides them to customize pain relief exercises or schedule a physiotherapy appointment based on their scores.
  • Streamlined communication and reduced costs with physiotherapists:
    The app records users' rehabilitation scores and transmits the data to the backend system, facilitating easy monitoring of user progress by physiotherapists.

Prototype

Testing & Feedback

Link the backgound data

Collaborated with physiotherapists and patients to conduct prototype testing during diagnosis and rehabilitation, encompassing the following key aspects:

  • Creation of user profiles.
  • Verification of displayed rehabilitation sets within the app.
  • Evaluation of the app's exercise videos.
  • Exercise with the video on the app.
  • Assessment of app-generated rehabilitation reminders.
  • Examination of outpatient records.

The findings from the testing revealed a remarkable 40% increase in rehabilitation efficiency for patients, alongside improved workflow efficiency for physiotherapists.
Notably, some patients expressed a desire for the app to feature articles providing correct physiotherapy knowledge, indicating a potential avenue for future development.
