Skin Analysis System Interface

UIUX Design - Research / Prototype

scroll

Concept

This application is an external project for the company, with the primary function being developing an application for dermatological skin analysis in medical aesthetic clinics. It requires integration with specialized equipment to scan and assess customers' skin conditions, enabling them to receive the most effective skincare or minor surgical procedures.

Roles

Prototype / Coding
In the project's initial phase, discussions were held with the client company's doctors to understand their specific requirements. The project manager then created a plan based on these requirements, and I visualized it. Simultaneously, based on the interview findings, we tailored the user interface to be user-friendly. After we built the mockup design, we handed it to the doctors for simulated testing and further adjustments based on their feedback.

Mockup

Design

The company's standard color, deep blue, was used for visual presentation. We complemented it with gray and white to avoid it feeling too heavy. Additionally, we applied gradients to the company's standard color to enhance the overall texture.
Since the product required ample screen space to display scan results, we simplified the layout of each page, allocating more space to the core product features.
We made several adjustments to the layout and experimented with various formatting options. Ultimately, we settled on a left-to-right layout for ease of use. This design choice aligns with doctors' habits, allowing them to select significant opportunities on the left and proceed from left to right within the content, reducing the risk of accidental selections. Block elements enable users to click on the desired content quickly. We also incorporated a feature for generating printable paper forms directly from the application, allowing doctors to provide patients with physical copies of their test results.
