Interview Podcast

Host - Research / Interview / Editor

Concept

"Tang Breaks the Silence" is a podcast interview program I launched on September 14, 2020, and is still actively updated. The program has produced over 50 episodes and has featured interviews with more than 30 guests. These interviews with individuals from various professions aim to allow them to share different lifestyles and perspectives on life.

Research

In these more than 50 episodes, we have interviewed over 30 guests, including musicians, doctors, voice actors, and even poets. To put it briefly, conducting meaningful interviews requires extensive preparation. Apart from researching relevant information about their respective professions, the most crucial aspect is understanding their current social issues and challenges. I typically prepare at least 15 questions, ensuring a clear interview agenda. I may jot down potential responses from the interviewees or how I might respond to their statements in advance.

Interview

"Chatting with friends" is my hosting style, where I don't provide explicit question prompts but subtly weave questions into the conversation, much like having a casual chat with friends. It's like tossing a conversational ball back and forth. After all, only some people are comfortable in front of a microphone. Hence, the aim is to make them feel at ease, facilitating relaxed and even heartfelt conversations and encouraging them to share more profound memories. I also avoid yes-or-no questions as much as possible, as they tend to limit responses and instead focus on questions that encourage more detailed replies.

Editing

For each episode, I invest a significant amount of time in editing. The process primarily involves removing verbal slips or repetitive phrases to enhance the listening experience. In addition to semantic post-production, I also incorporate appropriate intro music according to different series, aiming to create a sense of familiarity for regular listeners of the show.

Analysis

I host on a very convenient platform called SoundOn, which allows me to easily track the viewership numbers and conduct linear analysis of the episodes. It's an excellent channel for me to identify issues and make improvements.
